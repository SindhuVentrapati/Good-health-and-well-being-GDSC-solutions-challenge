Disease: Acne


Acne is a skin condition that occurs when your hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells. It causes whiteheads, blackheads or pimples.


PRECAUTIONS:

1.Keep your skin clean. Gently wash your face up to twice daily and after sweating. Choose a gentle, non-abrasive cleanser.
2.Choose the right skin care.
3.Shampoo regularly.

Disease: Hyperthermia


Hyperthermia is an abnormally high body temperature — or overheating. It's the opposite of hypothermia, when your body is too cold. Hyperthermia occurs when your body absorbs or generates more heat than it can release.


PRECAUTIONS:

1.Avoid strenuous physical activity in hot, humid conditions.
2.Consume sports drinks, lightly salted water or broth.
3.Never leave children in closed, hot spaces.


CONSULT:

a)General Physician

Disease: Chicken Pox


Chickenpox is an infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus. It causes an itchy rash with small, fluid-filled blisters. Chickenpox is highly contagious to people who haven't had the disease or been vaccinated against it.


PRECAUTIONS:

1.Spicy, salty, acidic, and crunchy foods should be avoided when you have chickenpox.
2.Drinking plenty of fluids — especially water — fights off dehydration. Dehydration can worsen your symptoms.


CONSULT:

a)Healthcare Provider

Disease: MEASLES


A condition transmitted in the same way as chickenpox, measles is another example of a childhood illness that spreads during summer. The rubeola virus causes measles, with symptoms that include dry cough, high fever, runny nose, and reddening of the eyes.


PRECAUTIONS:

1.Moisten the air. Use a humidifier to relieve a cough and sore throat.
2.Moisten your nose.
3.Rest your eyes.
